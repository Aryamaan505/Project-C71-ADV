Campus Life

Prayer Hall

Established in 2008

This building, which has been remodelled in 2008, was a local church where all locals used to visit on Sundays. The school authorities used several fund raising campaigns and successfully was able to remodel this. On Sundays St. Xavier's school is still open to all public to visit and pray in the Hall.

School Library

Built in 2011

The school library has a stock of about more than 15000 books categorized into "pre-primary", "primary", "middle" and "high" school. Many of the books are donations from children who bring them once a year on "Donation Drive" day from their homes.

School Auditorium

Built in 2009

The school auditorium was built to ensure a larger gathering of students can be accomodated during Assembly , Children Day celebrations, Teachers Day celebration, Annual oath taking ceremonies and any other such celebrations

School Achievements

Interschool debate on environment pollution
Won first Prize in inter school debate on environment pollution

We are proud to inform that students ( Adrika Chaudhary - 7C; Ayona Tandon - 7D; Aryamaan Trisal - 6J and Harpreet Singh - 6D) from the school received first prize in the "Environment pollution and it's solution" debate competition that was held in "The Oakwood High School" held in March '2021. The students were facilitated with a trophy and individual gift cheques of $100 each.


Interschool Football Competition
Came second in Inter School Football Competition

This year, the Football Competition was hosted by St. Xaviers and almost 11 school teams participated from the city. The shield was won by the St. John's Boys team and the home team came second.


Inter school Science Project competion
Won second prize in Inter school science project competition

The "Institute of Sciences", to promote the learning of science and new energies from young age itself, invited school teams in March to present a project on" Solar Energy and appliances". We are proud to mention that our school team comprising of 4 students from Grades 8 and 9 presented "Solar Traffic lights" that showcased a model. Their project based on presentation and uniqueness of idea was granted the second prize. Each of the 4 students have received a scholarship of $250
